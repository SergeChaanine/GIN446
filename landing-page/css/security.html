Security on the Web/Internet

Common Risks and Practical Defenses

Security on the Web covers threats that range from opportunistic phishing to sophisticated software supply-chain compromises. Threats affect everyone — users, small businesses, and large platforms — and often exploit human factors as well as technical weaknesses.

The internet has transformed communication, business, and daily life, but its openness also introduces risks. Individuals and organizations face threats ranging from phishing to ransomware. By understanding these risks and adopting safe practices, users can reduce exposure and safeguard digital assets.

Common Risks

  • Phishing: Deceptive emails and websites impersonating trusted sources (Jakobsson & Myers, 2006).
  • Malware & Ransomware: Malicious programs that steal, spy, or lock data until payment is made (Symantec, 2020).
  • Data Breaches: Exposed databases leaking sensitive information for misuse (Verizon, 2023).
  • Weak Credentials: Simple or reused passwords enabling unauthorized access.
  • Public Wi-Fi Risks: Attackers intercept data on unsecured networks.
  • Social Engineering: Psychological manipulation that exploits human trust.

Safe Practices

  1. Use strong, unique passwords with a password manager.
  2. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever available.
  3. Keep software updated to patch vulnerabilities.
  4. Be cautious with links and attachments in emails.
  5. Use HTTPS and VPNs for secure connections, especially on public Wi-Fi.
  6. Maintain regular backups to mitigate ransomware.
  7. Stay informed through cybersecurity awareness resources.
