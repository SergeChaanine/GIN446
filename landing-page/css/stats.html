Regional Internet Speed Overview

In many Middle Eastern countries, home WiFi speeds have been improving but still vary a lot. In the UAE, people often get very fast fixed broadband at home, with average speeds around 270 Mbps, while in Qatar speeds are usually above 130 Mbps. In Saudi Arabia, home connections in Riyadh average about 115 Mbps, giving households fairly strong WiFi when using modern routers. In Lebanon, average home internet speeds are lower: nationwide, fixed broadband speeds are usually around 30 Mbps, and in Beirut they can be a bit higher, though still below Gulf country levels. This gap shows the regional differences in internet infrastructure and service quality.
