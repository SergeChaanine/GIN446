How to read these numbers

DataReportal (Kepios) aggregates platform and survey data and reports country-level internet user estimates, while the World Bank/ITU provide development indicators often based on national surveys and administrative data. Speedtest (Ookla) measures network performance via active tests and reports median download speeds; these are useful for comparing measured user experience but depend on where and when tests were run. The References page provides direct links to the cited datasets.

Trends and context

Lebanon has seen rapid growth in internet adoption over the past decade, reaching over 90% of the population by early 2024 according to DataReportal. However, economic challenges and infrastructure issues have affected service quality and affordability. In the broader Middle East, there is a wide disparity between high-income Gulf states with near-universal access and fast networks, and lower-income countries where adoption and speeds lag behind global averages. Continued investment in infrastructure, regulatory reforms, and digital literacy programs are key to improving access and quality across the region.
